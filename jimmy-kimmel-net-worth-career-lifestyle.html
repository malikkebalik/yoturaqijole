Television host, comedian, producer, and writer best known for hosting a late-night talk show Jimmy Kimmel’s net worth is $50 million.

Kimmel is the executive producer of a late-night talk show Jimmy Kimmel live! Which premiered on ABC at Hollywood Masonic Temple in California on January 26, 2003.

Before producing and hosting his own talk show Kimmel was the co-host of Comedy Central’s The Man Show and Win Ben Stein’s Money.

Early Life

Jimmy Kimmel, originally James Christian Kimmel, was born on November the 13th, 1967, in Brooklyn, New York.

He was raised with his two youngest siblings in the neighborhood of Mill Basin.

His father, James John Kimmel, used to work at American Express and was an IBM executive.

His mother, Joan Iacono, is of Italian ancestry from Ischia, Naples.

Kimmel’s family moved to Las Vegas, Nevada, when he was nine years of age. He attended Ed W. Clark High School.

After graduating, he went to the University of Nevada, Las Vegas, for a year, and for his sophomore year, he transferred to Arizona State University.

In 2013 he received an honorary degree from the University of Nevada, Las Vegas.

Contract

Though Kimmel earns $15 million a year now, that was not the case before. Jimmy Kimmel was paid $10 million per year before his contract renewal in 2016.

Back in 2016, Kimmel extended his contract with ABC network for another three years till the fall of 2019 for $15 million per year, continuing his power as the longest-running host in late night.

The contract expired in 2019, and ABC offered Kimmel an additional three-year contract till 2022.

The number has not been disclosed about his salary and allowances, but we speculate at least $20 million or more.

Career

Though Jimmy is a well-known face on television now initially, he was not fond of working on Television. He was greatly inspired by David Letterman’s start in radio.

So he too started working in a radio station while he was still in high school as an unpaid employee.

He continued to work until he landed on first paying radio job in The me and Him show on KZOK-FM in Seattle.

He was a co-host on the show. However, that did not last long and he along with his co-host were fired from the show.

After that, he worked for KROQ-FM, and after five years working on the station, he began writing for Comedy Central. Soon he would appear in Comedy Central’s Win Ben Stein’s Money.

Likewise, he also hosted The Man show and left it in 2003 to start his own talk show Jimmy Kimmel Live. It’s 20 odd years later; he is one of the highest-paid talk show hosts in the world.
