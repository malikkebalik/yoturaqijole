Who is Joy Behar?

Joy Behar was born in 1942 as Josephine Victoria Occhiuto in Williamsburg, Brooklyn, the only child of an Italian-American Roman Catholic family. Her father, Gino Occhiuto, drove trucks for Coca-Cola, and her mother, Rose (née Carbone), was a seamstress.

Early in the 1980s, Behar began working in the entertainment industry as a receptionist before moving on to become a producer for Good Morning America. She performed stand-up comedy and made appearances on The New Show, a brief-lived Lorne Michaels NBC production, as well as Good Morning America on ABC. She was the host of a variety discussion program on Lifetime Television in 1987 called Way Off Broadway, which featured writer and performer Larry David.

Barbara Walters and Behar co-created the ABC daytime chat show The View, and Behar joined the panel as one of the founding members in 1997. Behar first appeared exclusively on days when Walters wasn’t available, but eventually, she was added as a regular co-host.

Following more than a decade of nominations for the program, Behar and the four co-hosts, Whoopi Goldberg, Elisabeth Hasselbeck, Sherri Shepherd, and Barbara Walters, won the Daytime Emmy Award for Outstanding Talk Show Host in August 2009. Behar continued to serve as a guest co-host in 2014 and 2015 after leaving in 2013. On August 25, 2015, ABC revealed that Behar would return as a regular co-host beginning on September 8, 2015, with the 19th season’s debut.

She started filling in as a guest host on Larry King Live on occasion in 2007.
